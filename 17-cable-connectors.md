# Cable Connectors

## Main idea

Cable connectors are used to terminate and connect copper or coaxial cables to network devices, modems, telephones, and other communication equipment. Different connectors are used for different cable types and network purposes.

## RJ11 connector

RJ11 stands for Registered Jack 11.

Features:

* Commonly used for telephone connections
* Often used with DSL connections
* Usually has 6 positions and 2 conductors
* Smaller than an RJ45 connector

RJ11 is mostly associated with voice and telephone-style connections.

## RJ45 connector

RJ45 is commonly used for Ethernet networking.

Features:

* Used with twisted-pair Ethernet cables
* Common connector for LAN connections
* Often described as 8P8C
* 8P8C means 8 positions and 8 conductors
* Modular connector design

RJ45 connectors are commonly used with Cat5e, Cat6, and Cat6a Ethernet cables.

## F-connector

F-connectors are used with coaxial cables.

Common uses:

* Cable TV
* Cable modems
* Broadband internet over coaxial cable
* DOCSIS networks

DOCSIS stands for Data Over Cable Service Interface Specification. It is used by cable internet providers to deliver internet service over coaxial cable networks.

F-connectors are threaded, which helps keep the connection secure.

## BNC connector

BNC stands for Bayonet Neill-Concelman.

Features:

* Used with coaxial cables
* Uses a twist-lock bayonet mechanism
* Provides a secure connection
* Common in video, radio, and older networking applications
* Can be used with some twinax and DS3 WAN links

The twist-lock design helps prevent accidental disconnection.

## Key takeaway

Different cable connectors are used for different communication systems. RJ11 is used for telephone and DSL connections, RJ45 is used for Ethernet, F-connectors are common in cable TV and cable internet, and BNC connectors are used for secure coaxial connections in video, radio, and some network applications.
