# Copper Cabling

## Main idea

Copper cabling is one of the most important parts of a network. Even wireless networks eventually connect back to wired infrastructure, so cable planning and installation are important for reliable network performance.

## Importance of cabling

Network cabling should be planned carefully because it can be difficult and expensive to replace later. Poor cable installation can cause performance issues, troubleshooting problems, and messy network environments.

Good cabling should be:

* Organized
* Properly labeled
* Installed according to standards
* Suitable for the required speed and distance
* Protected from interference and physical damage

## Twisted-pair copper cables

Twisted-pair cables are commonly used in Ethernet networks. They contain pairs of copper wires twisted together.

The twists help reduce:

* Electromagnetic interference
* Crosstalk between wires
* Signal degradation

Each pair may have a different twist rate to reduce interference between pairs inside the same cable.

## Cable categories and speed

Copper cables are classified into categories such as Cat5e, Cat6, and Cat6a. The cable category helps determine what speeds and distances the cable can reliably support.

The cable itself does not create speed. It provides the physical medium that carries electrical signals. Higher-speed Ethernet standards usually require higher-quality cable categories and proper installation.

Examples:

* Cat5e is commonly used for Gigabit Ethernet
* Cat6 can support higher speeds over shorter distances
* Cat6a is commonly used for 10 Gigabit Ethernet over longer copper runs

## Coaxial cables

Coaxial cables have conductors that share a common axis. They are commonly used for television, cable internet, and broadband connections.

A common coaxial cable type is RG-6.

Cable internet commonly uses a standard called DOCSIS, which stands for Data Over Cable Service Interface Specification. (high speed internet over cable)

## Twinaxial cables

Twinaxial cables, or twinax cables, contain two inner conductors. They are commonly used for short-distance high-speed connections, such as 10 Gigabit Ethernet with SFP+ direct attach cables.

Advantages:

* Low cost for short distances
* Low latency
* Common in data centers and network racks
* Supports full-duplex communication

Full duplex means data can be sent and received at the same time.

A limitation of twinax cables is distance. They are usually used for short cable runs, often only a few meters. (around 2)

## Plenum space

Plenum space is the area used for air circulation, such as the space above a drop ceiling. Network cables may pass through this space.

Because air moves through plenum spaces, fire safety is important. Plenum-rated cables are designed to produce less smoke and fewer harmful fumes during a fire.

Cable planning in plenum spaces should consider:

* Fire safety rating
* Cable organization
* Airflow
* Bend radius
* Future maintenance

## Bend radius

Bend radius refers to how sharply a cable can be bent without damaging it or affecting signal quality. Cables should not be bent too tightly because this can reduce performance or cause connection problems.

## Key takeaway

Copper cabling is still fundamental in networking. Twisted-pair copper is common for Ethernet, coaxial cable is used for cable TV and internet, and twinax is useful for short high-speed data center connections. Good cable planning, proper cable category selection, and safe installation are important for reliable networks.
