# Optical Fiber Cables

## Main idea

Optical fiber cables transmit data using light instead of electrical signals. They are commonly used for high-speed networking, long-distance communication, and environments where radio or electromagnetic interference can be a problem.

## Types of fiber

### Multimode fiber

Multimode fiber allows multiple paths of light to travel through the fiber core.

* Used for shorter distances (around like 2km or so)
* Has a larger core
* Common in buildings, campuses, and data centers
* Often uses LEDs or lower-cost light sources

### Single-mode fiber

Single-mode fiber allows one main path of light to travel through the fiber core.

* Used for longer distances
* Has a smaller core
* Common in long-distance and high-speed network links
* Usually uses lasers as the light source

## Fiber cable parts

An optical fiber cable has several important parts:

* Core: the center part where the light signal travels
* Cladding: surrounds the core and helps keep the light inside the fiber
* Buffer coating: protects the glass fiber from damage
* Ferrule: part of the connector that holds and aligns the fiber end

## Why fiber is used

Optical fiber is useful because:

* It supports high bandwidth
* It can transmit data over long distances
* The signal degrades slowly compared to many copper connections
* It is immune to radio and electromagnetic interference
* It is harder to tap or monitor compared to copper cabling

## Key takeaway

Multimode fiber is usually used for shorter distances, while single-mode fiber is used for longer distances. Fiber is reliable, fast, resistant to interference, and useful for high-speed network connections.
