# Fiber Connectors

## Main idea

Fiber connectors are used to connect fiber optic cables to network devices, patch panels, and transceivers. Different connector types have different sizes, locking mechanisms, and use cases.

## SC connector

SC can stand for Subscriber Connector, Standard Connector, or Square Connector.

Features:

* Common fiber connector
* Square-shaped connector body
* Push-pull locking mechanism
* Used in many fiber network installations

## LC connector

LC can stand for Lucent Connector, Local Connector, or Light Connector.

Features:

* Smaller than SC
* Compact design
* Common in modern networking equipment
* Often used with SFP and SFP+ transceivers
* Uses a latch-style push-pull mechanism

LC connectors are popular because they save space, especially in switches, patch panels, and data center environments.

## MPO connector

MPO stands for Multi-fiber Push-On.

Features:

* Supports multiple fiber strands in one connector
* Common versions can contain 12 fibers or more
* Uses a push-on connection design
* Useful for high-density fiber connections
* Common in data centers and high-speed networking

MTP is a branded version of MPO made by US Conec.

## Key takeaway

Fiber connectors allow fiber cables to be connected cleanly and reliably to network equipment. SC is a common square push-pull connector, LC is a smaller compact connector often used with SFP modules, and MPO supports multiple fibers in one connector for high-density networking.
